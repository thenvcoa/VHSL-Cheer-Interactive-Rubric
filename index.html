Key Notes & Additional Info
Important additional information that applies across both the Stunt and Pyramid rubrics. These rules affect how difficulty is scored and where skills land within a range.

Front Spots

Front spots (stunt groups of more than 4) or less than full participation from the maximum number of possible stunt groups will decrease the value of a skill within the range.

Body Positions

IBP: Liberty, Hitch, Torch
ABP: Heel Stretch, Arabesque, Scorpion, Bow & Arrow, etc.

Body positions alone do not receive credit for difficulty — they must be connected to a skill/transition.

Momentum Through IBP

Momentum from a skill/transition directly into an ABP may pass through lib/platform/dangle, but must be continuous without pause in order to be credited together.

Dismounts & Transitions

If a transition or dismount meets the requirement of a rubric skill, it should be given credit as such. Don't overlook transitions — they count!

Inversions

Flyer's shoulders are below their waist, regardless of foot placement. Inverted transitions must remain inverted at the initiation of the bases' movement to be credited.

Single Base Skills

Must begin from the ground/load, end, and maintain throughout the skill a single base (plus spotter where required). Skills that start as full group and release to single base do not qualify.

Pyramid Bracers (1 or 2)

The number in parentheses indicates how many bracers are used. Unbraced skills or those using fewer bracers receive more credit within the same range.

Unbraced Pyramid Timing

Unbraced pyramid transitions must connect at the top of the skill within 2 counts to be considered a pyramid and receive pyramid credit.

Ripples

Ripples are allowed within the stunt/pyramid sequence but should be clearly defined. It must be very obvious that athletes are not being recycled.

Pyramid Variety

In pyramid, a variety of flyers involved in transitional skills may increase score within the range and/or be given consideration in overall impression.

Full Up/Around Variations

For all levels, the variation or modified grip version of the skill will be scored lower in the range than the true/traditional version.

Twisting — Flyer Rotation

With the exception of ¼ twisting releases, the majority of the rotation must be completed by the flyer. Bases walking a stunt around does not constitute a full up.

Key Majority Rules
Ripples Are Allowed
Ripples (sequential timing of the same exact skill) are allowed within stunt, pyramid, and tumbling sequences but should be clearly defined. It should be very obvious that athletes are not being recycled.
Front Spots & Non-Participation
Front spots (stunt groups of more than 4) or less than full participation from the maximum number of possible stunt groups will decrease the value of a skill within the range.
Pyramid Majority (16+ Athletes)
Teams of 16 or more are required to have at least TWO different flyers (in addition to any bracers) completing the required rubric skills. These may be a connected 2x2 pyramid, two separate mini pyramids, or any combination. Ripples are allowed but should be obvious and clearly defined.
If Majority Fails
If a required skill falls before completion, judges look for other transition/lower-level skills. If skills only occur in different ranges without meeting minimums for one range, the score drops to the lowest range executed. If no majority at all → score drops to the 0–1 range.
